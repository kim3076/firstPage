Agricultural damage

South Korea has a minimum of 20% to 80% damage in each agricultural area, with low temperatures and frost falling to minus 4 degrees Celsius in April 2020. In addition, there was and is a significant impact on the fishery, especially in the South Jeon-Nam and Gwangju regions in the early 2000 and amount of damages was estimated to be 20 billion Korean won (200,000,000 in US dollars).

Aquaculture damage

The damage hit 2017, as the sea temperature dropped in the recent cold wave, causing fish to be killed in the farm. Jeonnam Yeosu City recently announced that 400,000 fish, including chamdoms, were killed in 11 farms in Dolsan County due to the cold and the breeding volume of these fish was 670,000, almost 60% of the fish have been killed.

Casualties

According to the Ministry of Health, S. Korea 2019, the number of casualties caused by the cold wave has increased in the last five years. From 258 in 2013 to 458 in 2014, 483 in 2015, 441 in 2016, and 631 in 2017. In particular, the damage was concentrated on the age group in the 60s and older.
